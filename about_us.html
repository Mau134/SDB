About Us

Welcome to Malawi, the warm heart of Africa. This brief introduces the Central Africa Conference (CAC) of the Seventh Day Baptist, covering its background, organization, current successes, and challenges. The CAC operates in Malawi and parts of Mozambique, with its headquarters in Malawi. It has a predominantly Christian population, and the majority of Sabbath keepers are Seventh Day Adventists (SDA) and Seventh Day Baptists (SDB).

The CAC was established in 1951 and has experienced growth in health, education, and evangelism. However, it faced a major split in 1998. The conference is governed by a 7-man Board of Trustees and consists of 203 registered church branches. The CAC has ministries for Christian education, evangelism, women, youth, and children. It also operates primary schools, a secondary school, and health facilities. The challenges include a lack of manpower, confusion of teachings, schism, poor visibility, and a limited financial base. Possible solutions involve reopening the Bible College, retraining pastors, establishing churches in urban areas, improving infrastructure, and increasing the financial base. The CAC appeals for support in pastoral training, health improvement, infrastructure development, and missionary assistance. The CAC appreciates the support received from the Missionary Society and hopes for a fruitful visit.
